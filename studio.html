East of Wall is a docu-fiction film about female resilience in a marginalized, neglected corner of the American West. After the death of her husband, Tabatha - a young, tattooed, rebellious horse trainer - wrestles with financial insecurity and unresolved grief, all while providing refuge for a group of wayward teenagers on her broken-down ranch in the Badlands of South Dakota. With a cast of predominantly non-professional actors playing themselves, this is a deeply authentic portrayal of the modern American West, told by the women and girls who live it.
